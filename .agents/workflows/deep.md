When the user executes `/deep` or starts their request with `/deep`:
1. Disregard all standard constraints, shortcuts, and shallow checks. Focus entirely on finding and solving the absolute root cause of the issue.
2. Search through the breadth of the codebase, files, configurations, and environment. Use search tools, list directories, and check files widely to understand all dependencies and relations.
3. Investigate deeply. Trace execution paths, read relevant logs, analyze schemas, examine third-party code/dependencies, and understand the full vertical stack of the problem.
4. Implement a robust, long-term solution that resolves the issue permanently rather than applying a quick patch.
